## What the Audit Found

The scan detected 12 issues across all four dimensions. These five represent the highest-impact conversion killers — the ones costing the most leads per day.

| Issue | Severity | Score Impact | Category |
|-------|----------|-------------|----------|
| No portfolio or case studies on any page | Critical | −18 pts | Conversion |
| Copyright year stuck at 2020 — no content updated in 6+ years | Critical | −15 pts | Design & Trust |
| Zero blog, articles, or educational content | High | −12 pts | Competitor |
| No lead capture form, live chat, or sticky CTA on any page | High | −10 pts | Conversion |
| Generic meta title/description across all pages (duplicate, under-length) | Medium | −6 pts | Technical |

**Total score impact of addressed issues:** 61 points (projected recovery to ~80–85/100)

### Deeper Context on Each Finding

**1. No portfolio or case studies (Critical −18)**
A web design agency without a portfolio is like a restaurant without a menu. Law firms shopping for a marketing partner look for: *"Who else have you worked with? What results did you get? What do your sites actually look like?"* Denver Legal Marketing's site offers none of this. Every prospect that lands on the page hits a wall of vague claims with zero proof.

**2. Copyright stuck at 2020 — 6+ years stale (Critical −15)**
As of 2026, a copyright footer reading "© 2020" signals abandonment. For a marketing agency that claims to provide ongoing digital services, this single detail destroys credibility. Law firms — meticulous by nature and adversarial by training — notice this immediately. If the agency can't maintain their own site, how would they maintain yours?

**3. Zero content marketing (High −12)**
No blog, no articles, no guides, no resources. For a legal marketing agency, this is a competitive handicap of the first order. Legal is one of the most content-driven verticals in digital marketing — law firms rank on authority, and authority is built through published expertise. Denver Legal Marketing's site communicates no expertise at all.

**4. No lead capture anywhere (High −10)**
The site has no contact form on any page. No "Schedule a Consultation" button. No sticky CTA. No live chat. No phone number in the hero. The primary conversion action a prospect can take is… scrolling. And then leaving.

**5. Duplicate, generic meta tags (Medium −6)**
Every page on the site uses a near-identical meta title and description — short, keyword-stuffed, and irrelevant to the specific page content. Google sees this as thin content. Local competitors with proper on-page SEO outrank Denver Legal Marketing for its own target terms.

## Key Takeaways for OA Strategy

1. **The irony angle converts.** A service provider whose own digital presence contradicts their value proposition is the highest-probability outreach target. Denver Legal Marketing's 39/100 score is an indictment of their own service quality that no competitor can refute.

2. **Score recovery is dramatic from the floor.** Low-scoring sites see the largest percentage improvements — 39 → 85 looks like a 115% increase. That's a more compelling before/after than 72 → 90.

3. **The fix is replicable.** The improvement pattern (portfolio + content + forms + metadata) is the same playbook that applies to most local service business audits. This case study, once published, becomes a template for the vertical-messaging approach.

4. **Content is the long game that pays immediately.** A legal marketing agency without content is a contradiction. Creating a content strategy for them creates a recurring engagement model — blog management, SEO monitoring, monthly reporting — beyond the initial site fix.

## Specific Site Findings Used in Outreach

1. **No online booking or scheduling** — For a pest control company that offers same-day service and two-hour arrival windows, there's no way for a customer to schedule a service visit online. Every visitor must call during business hours.

2. **Contact form is basic Gravity Form — no lead qualification** — The /contact-us/ page has a basic form with no service selector, no urgency filter, no pest-type dropdown. Every lead is treated identically regardless of whether they have bed bugs, raccoons, or routine ant prevention.

3. **Pest list is just text — no detail pages** — The site lists 8 pest types (ants, bed bugs, beetles, birds, rodents, cockroaches, flies, raccoon) but each is a simple heading with no service page, treatment explanation, or pest-specific landing content.

4. **No instant quote or pricing transparency** — Despite serving residential, commercial, AND industrial customers, there is no pricing guidance, package comparison, or estimate request flow. Visitors guessing about cost will leave.

5. **Gmail-style contact email on domain** — denverpestcontrol@denverpestcontrol.com is fine technically, but the contact page uses it as a mailto link rather than pushing through a structured intake form → CRM pipeline.

6. **Heavy reliance on phone-only conversions** — With 30+ glowing reviews on the site, none of them are connected to a booking CTA. Each testimonial section leads to a dead end — no "get the same service" button, no schedule link.

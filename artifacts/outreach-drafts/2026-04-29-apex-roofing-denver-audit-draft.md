## Specific Site Findings Used in Outreach

1. **No online inspection booking or estimate request** — Despite long-form content that positions them as storm response and insurance claim experts, there is no way for a homeowner to schedule a roof inspection online. For a category where timing matters (storm damage = water intrusion in hours), this is a critical gap.

2. **No emergency storm response CTA above the fold** — Apex markets 24-hour storm response and "within hours" emergency tarping, but the homepage loads with content about Denver's hail corridor before showing anything a homeowner in crisis can click. The storm response message is buried below 2000+ words of educational content.

3. **Content length creates browsing friction** — The homepage alone is enormous — multiple 500+ word paragraphs about Denver microclimates, elevation specs, and insulation science. While this helps SEO, it hurts conversion: visitors in need of an emergency roof repair can't find a CTA without scrolling past thousands of words.

4. **No insurance claim intake flow** — Their main competitive angle is navigating insurance claims and fighting lowball adjuster estimates. But the site offers no way for a homeowner to upload photos, describe damage, or start a claim intake through the website.

5. **Service page is a single page with header-based sections** — All services (emergency repair, replacement, inspections, commercial, etc.) are on ONE page with anchor links. No dedicated service landing pages means no targeted SEO for specific search queries like "emergency roof leak repair Denver" or "hail damage inspection Lakewood."

6. **No review aggregation or social proof on homepage** — Despite being in business long enough to have a track record, there are zero customer reviews, ratings, or BBB information visible anywhere on the homepage or service pages.

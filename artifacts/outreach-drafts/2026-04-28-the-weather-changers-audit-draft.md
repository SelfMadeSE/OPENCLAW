## Specific Site Findings Used in Outreach

1. **No online booking or scheduling** — For an HVAC company, especially during peak seasons (summer A/C, winter heating), customers want to book service calls online. The site relies entirely on phone and contact form.

2. **Coupon/promotion as primary CTA** — The main call-to-action is centered on coupons rather than emergency service, tune-up scheduling, or system installation. This positions the business as a discount option rather than a trusted service provider.

3. **No emergency callout prominence** — HVAC emergencies (no heat, broken A/C in summer) are high-urgency, high-value calls. The site doesn't feature an emergency service CTA above the fold.

4. **No live chat or callback request** — Visitors who need quick answers can't use chat or request a callback. This means the site can't capture leads during off-hours or weekends.

5. **No response time guarantee** — Competitors in the Denver HVAC market increasingly advertise response time guarantees. Missing this creates a competitive disadvantage.
